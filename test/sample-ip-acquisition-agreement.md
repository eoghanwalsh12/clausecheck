# INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ACQUISITION AGREEMENT

**THIS INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY ACQUISITION AGREEMENT** (this "Agreement") is entered into as of the 15th day of March, 2026 (the "Effective Date"),

**BY AND BETWEEN:**

**MERIDIAN TECHNOLOGIES, INC.**, a Delaware corporation, with its principal place of business at 2400 Sand Hill Road, Suite 300, Menlo Park, California 94025 (hereinafter referred to as "Acquirer" or "Meridian");

**AND**

**NEXTERA AI SOLUTIONS LTD.**, a company incorporated under the laws of Ireland, with its registered office at 42 Barrow Street, Grand Canal Dock, Dublin 4, D04 V2N9, Ireland (hereinafter referred to as "Seller" or "Nextera");

(each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties").

## RECITALS

**WHEREAS**, Nextera is engaged in the business of developing and commercialising artificial intelligence and machine learning software, platforms, and related technologies (the "Business");

**WHEREAS**, Nextera owns or controls certain intellectual property rights, including but not limited to patents, patent applications, trade secrets, proprietary algorithms, software source code, training datasets, and associated know-how, as more particularly described herein and in the schedules annexed hereto;

**WHEREAS**, Meridian desires to acquire from Nextera, and Nextera desires to sell, assign, transfer, and convey to Meridian, all of Nextera's right, title, and interest in and to the Acquired IP (as defined below), subject to the terms and conditions set forth in this Agreement;

**WHEREAS**, the acquisition of the Acquired IP is being made in connection with, but is not contingent upon, the broader strategic transaction between the Parties contemplated under that certain Asset Purchase Agreement of even date herewith (the "APA"), except as otherwise expressly provided herein;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants, agreements, representations, and warranties contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE 2 — ASSIGNMENT AND TRANSFER OF ACQUIRED IP

**2.1 Assignment.** Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Nextera hereby irrevocably sells, assigns, transfers, conveys, and delivers to Meridian, and Meridian hereby purchases and accepts from Nextera, all of Nextera's right, title, and interest in and to the Acquired IP, free and clear of all Encumbrances, except for Permitted Encumbrances (as defined in Section 2.3).

**2.2 Instruments of Transfer.** At the Closing (as defined in Article 3), Nextera shall execute and deliver to Meridian:

(a) a Patent Assignment substantially in the form of Exhibit A hereto, covering all Assigned Patents;

(b) a Copyright Assignment substantially in the form of Exhibit B hereto, covering all copyrights in the Software Assets;

(c) a Trademark Assignment substantially in the form of Exhibit C hereto, covering all Trademarks;

(d) a Trade Secret Assignment and Non-Disclosure Agreement substantially in the form of Exhibit D hereto;

(e) such other bills of sale, assignments, certificates of title, and other instruments of transfer or conveyance as Meridian may reasonably request to evidence or effect the transfer of the Acquired IP.

**2.3 Permitted Encumbrances.** The Acquired IP shall be transferred subject only to the following Permitted Encumbrances:

(a) the licence-back granted to Nextera pursuant to Article 5;

(b) existing non-exclusive licences to third parties identified in Schedule F, provided that such licences are non-assignable and terminable in accordance with their terms;

(c) licences of Open Source Materials incorporated within the Software Assets, to the extent identified in Schedule G; and

(d) any Encumbrances arising solely from acts of Meridian or its Affiliates.

**2.4 Excluded Assets.** Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this Agreement, the Acquired IP shall not include: (a) Nextera's general corporate intellectual property not specifically used in or developed for the Business; (b) Nextera's name, logos, and branding (except as specifically included in the Trademarks); and (c) the items listed in Schedule H (the "Excluded Assets").

## ARTICLE 3 — CLOSING; PURCHASE PRICE

**3.1 Closing.** The closing of the transactions contemplated by this Agreement (the "Closing") shall take place remotely via electronic exchange of signatures on the third (3rd) business day after all conditions precedent set forth in Article 9 have been satisfied or waived, or at such other time, date, and place as the Parties may mutually agree in writing (the "Closing Date").

**3.2 Purchase Price.** In consideration for the sale, assignment, and transfer of the Acquired IP, Meridian shall pay to Nextera an aggregate purchase price of ONE HUNDRED AND SEVENTY-FIVE MILLION UNITED STATES DOLLARS (US$175,000,000) (the "Purchase Price"), allocated as follows:

(a) **Cash Consideration:** US$120,000,000, payable by wire transfer of immediately available funds to an account designated by Nextera, to be paid as follows:

   (i) US$90,000,000 at Closing;

   (ii) US$15,000,000 on the first anniversary of the Closing Date, subject to the satisfaction of the Milestone Conditions set forth in Section 3.4(a); and

   (iii) US$15,000,000 on the second anniversary of the Closing Date, subject to the satisfaction of the Milestone Conditions set forth in Section 3.4(b);

(b) **Equity Consideration:** 2,500,000 shares of Meridian common stock (the "Consideration Shares"), valued at US$22.00 per share as of the Effective Date, representing an aggregate equity value of US$55,000,000, subject to the lock-up provisions of Section 3.5.

**3.3 Escrow.** At Closing, US$17,500,000 of the Cash Consideration (the "Escrow Amount") shall be deposited into an escrow account (the "Escrow Account") established with JPMorgan Chase Bank, N.A. as escrow agent (the "Escrow Agent"), pursuant to the terms of an escrow agreement substantially in the form of Exhibit E (the "Escrow Agreement"). The Escrow Amount shall be available to satisfy any indemnification claims by Meridian under Article 11, and any remaining balance shall be released to Nextera on the date that is eighteen (18) months after the Closing Date, less any amounts subject to pending claims.

**3.4 Milestone Payments.** The deferred Cash Consideration described in Sections 3.2(a)(ii) and 3.2(a)(iii) shall be contingent upon the following:

(a) **First Milestone:** Nextera shall have completed the full transfer and integration of all Software Assets source code repositories, build environments, CI/CD pipelines, and associated documentation to Meridian's designated systems within twelve (12) months of the Closing Date, as certified by Meridian's Chief Technology Officer (acting reasonably);

(b) **Second Milestone:** All key technical employees of Nextera identified in Schedule I (the "Key Employees") shall have remained continuously employed by Meridian or its designee for a period of not less than twenty-four (24) months following the Closing Date, with a minimum retention of seventy-five percent (75%) of such Key Employees.

**3.5 Lock-Up Period.** The Consideration Shares shall be subject to the following transfer restrictions:

(a) For a period of twelve (12) months following the Closing Date (the "Lock-Up Period"), Nextera shall not, directly or indirectly, sell, transfer, assign, pledge, hypothecate, or otherwise dispose of any Consideration Shares, except: (i) transfers to Affiliates of Nextera, provided such Affiliates agree in writing to be bound by the terms of this Section 3.5; or (ii) transfers with the prior written consent of Meridian.

(b) Following the expiration of the Lock-Up Period, Nextera may sell Consideration Shares, provided that Nextera shall not sell more than the greater of (i) 250,000 shares or (ii) ten percent (10%) of the then-outstanding Consideration Shares in any single calendar quarter, without the prior written consent of Meridian (not to be unreasonably withheld).

**3.6 Purchase Price Allocation.** The Parties shall cooperate to allocate the Purchase Price among the Acquired IP in accordance with Section 1060 of the United States Internal Revenue Code and the Treasury Regulations thereunder. Such allocation shall be set forth in Schedule J, which shall be agreed upon by the Parties within sixty (60) days following the Closing Date. Each Party shall report the transactions contemplated by this Agreement for all Tax purposes in a manner consistent with such allocation and shall not take any position inconsistent therewith in any Tax Return, refund claim, litigation, or otherwise, unless required by applicable law.

## ARTICLE 7 — NON-COMPETITION AND NON-SOLICITATION

**7.1 Non-Competition.** During the period commencing on the Closing Date and ending on the fourth (4th) anniversary thereof (the "Restricted Period"), Nextera shall not, and shall cause its Affiliates not to, directly or indirectly, engage in, invest in, own, manage, operate, finance, control, or participate in the ownership, management, operation, financing, or control of any business that develops, markets, sells, or licenses products or services that are competitive with the Acquired IP or the products and services of Meridian that incorporate the Acquired IP, in any jurisdiction worldwide (the "Restricted Activity"); provided, however, that:

(a) the foregoing shall not prohibit Nextera from owning, solely as a passive investor, less than five percent (5%) of the outstanding shares of any class of securities of a publicly traded company; and

(b) the foregoing shall not restrict Nextera from conducting the Retained Business in accordance with the licence-back granted under Article 5.

**7.2 Non-Solicitation of Employees.** During the Restricted Period, Nextera shall not, and shall cause its Affiliates not to, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, hire, or attempt to solicit, recruit, or hire any Key Employee or any other employee of Meridian who was previously employed by Nextera and became an employee of Meridian in connection with the transactions contemplated hereby or the APA; provided that general solicitations of employment not specifically targeted at such employees shall not be deemed a violation of this Section 7.2.

**7.4 Enforceability.** The Parties acknowledge and agree that the covenants contained in this Article 7 are reasonable and necessary to protect the legitimate business interests of Meridian. If any court of competent jurisdiction shall determine that any provision of this Article 7 is unenforceable because it is overbroad in scope, duration, or geographic area, such court shall have the power to reduce the scope, duration, or geographic area of such provision, and in its reduced form, such provision shall be enforceable.
